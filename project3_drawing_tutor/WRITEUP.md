# Engineering Drawing Tutor — Technical Write-Up

## Overview

The Engineering Drawing Tutor is a fine-tuned Gemma 4 E4B model that reads
engineering drawings, generates manufacturing checklists, explains tolerances,
and flags AS1100 compliance issues. It targets apprentice machinists and vocational
students in developing countries who receive complex drawings they cannot fully
interpret without expert guidance.

## Motivation

In vocational training programs across Southeast Asia, Africa, and South America,
apprentices routinely receive AS1100 or ISO 128 drawings but have no access to
instructors who can explain GD&T symbols, surface finish callouts, or tolerance
requirements. A pocket-sized offline tutor changes this equation fundamentally.

This directly addresses the **Education in Underserved Communities / Workforce
Upskilling** theme.

## Technical Approach

### Data Pipeline

Two data sources were combined:

**AS1100 Engineering Drawings** (HuggingFace jcrzd, CC-BY-4.0): 24 annotated
drawings with pre-formatted QA pairs covering AS1100 compliance review, title block
completeness, and error identification. These 24 pairs serve as high-quality gold
examples.

**Drawing Annotation Recognition** (Roboflow, CC-BY-4.0): YOLO-format annotations
distinguishing dimension tags (`dt`) from text labels. Generates QA pairs about
annotation counts and manufacturing checklists.

**Synthetic template pairs** (1,476 pairs): 10 curated question-answer templates
covering GD&T fundamentals, AS1100 conventions, tolerance reading, projection
standards, and title block requirements. Generated by random sampling to pad the
dataset to 1,500 total pairs.

**Final dataset:** 1,500 QA pairs (1,200 train + 300 eval).

### Model

- **Base:** `google/gemma-4-e4b-it` (4B edge instruct)
- **Method:** LoRA fine-tuning via Unsloth (r=16, α=32, dropout=0.05)
- **Quantization:** 4-bit NF4 during training
- **Hardware:** Kaggle T4 (15 GB VRAM, free tier)
- **Training:** 3 epochs, batch size 2 + gradient accumulation 8

### Evaluation

Automatic evaluation uses Exact Match on dimension value queries and Token F1 on
checklist and explanation outputs. Human rubric evaluates checklist completeness
and accuracy on 15 held-out drawings (5-point scale).

## Offline Capability

GGUF 4-bit export enables inference in a workshop with no internet, using a Raspberry
Pi or any x86 laptop — putting AS1100 expertise in a machinist's pocket.
